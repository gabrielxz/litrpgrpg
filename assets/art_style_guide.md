# LitRPG TTRPG Art Style Guide

**Concept:** A hybrid of gritty, urban street-art stencils and the frantic, raw energy of Yoji Shinkawa's sumi-e ink wash. The goal is to completely avoid the smooth, cartoony, and polished "plastic" sheen typical of standard anime rendering, replacing it with something handcrafted, chaotic, and heavily textured.

## 1. Linework & Rendering
*   **Thick & Energetic:** Use bold, heavy black strokes that imitate a stencil-like appearance, but break the clean lines with chaotic, expressive brushstrokes.
*   **No Smooth Shading:** Avoid soft gradients. Form and shadow should be defined by frantic ink splatters, unrefined sketch lines, and heavy black negative space.
*   **Raw & Unfinished:** Parts of the illustration should look like a hasty, adrenaline-fueled sketch, leaving some edges raw or bleeding into the background.

## 2. Color Palette
*   **Muted & Desaturated:** The world is grim and grounded. Rely heavily on faded blues, grays, dirty browns, and dried-blood reds.
*   **Abstract Washes:** Apply color not as strict "fill," but as loose, abstract watercolor or ink washes that bleed outside the linework.
*   **The System Pop:** Use rare, highly vibrant neon colors (like electric blue or arcane purple) *only* for System elements, magic, or holographic screens to make them starkly contrast against the gritty world.

## 3. Texture & Detail
*   **Distressed Mediums:** Overlay the entire image with rough, physical textures. Think scratched metal, worn paint, cracked concrete, or distressed, aged paper.
*   **Ink & Grime:** Heavy use of ink splatters, drops, and chaotic smudges that make the piece feel like it was painted in a rush on a dirty wall or ruined parchment.

## 4. Character Features
*   **Stylized, Not Cartoony:** Maintain highly stylized anime proportions and dynamic, gravity-defying poses, but completely strip away the "cute" or polished rendering.
*   **Expressive but Gritty:** Eyes should be sharp, fierce, and expressive, but avoid the generic "shiny" anime eye reflections.
*   **Hair & Gear:** Incorporate typical anime features like exaggerated bangs or flowing strands, but render them with jagged, rugged brushstrokes. Gear should look worn, mismatched, and heavily used.

## 5. Visual Accents & Environment
*   **Urban Decay:** Incorporate wear-and-tear marks, deep stains, and grime on characters and their surroundings.
*   **Street-Art Aesthetic:** Backgrounds should feature abstract, graffiti-like elements (splatters and stenciled shapes) without relying on legible words, evoking an underground or post-apocalyptic punk vibe.
